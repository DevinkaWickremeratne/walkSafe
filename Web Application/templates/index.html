Background

There are over 996, 939 vision-impaired people living in Sri Lanka. 1.5% of them suffer from total blindness.

The vision-impaired community face many difficulties in travelling through public-spaces, due to the various types of obstacles they may encounter. The white cane has limited functionality and can only safeguard the vision-impaired user to an extent.

The WalkSafe Smart Cane aims to make public space travelling a safer, more independent experience for the Sri Lankan vision-impaired community.

  • Identifies Nature of Obstacle

    The WalkSafe Smart Cane combines sensors with computer vision techniques and identifies obstacles and alerts the vision-impaired user in real-time. The system is able to detect obstacles such as broken pavement blocks, drains, poles, man-holes and parked vehicles.

  • Mobile and Web Applications

    The vision-impaired user's guardian can monitor the user's movements through the mobile/web application. The uer's location & the real-time view of his/her surroundings can be veiwed through the application.

  • Emergency Alert Feature

    Whenever the user feels that he/she is an emergency situation, a SOS alert can be activated throguh the smart cane & will be directly sent to the guardian's mobile/web application.
